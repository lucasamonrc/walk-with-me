"Hear Him"

"(...) — This is My Beloved Son. Hear Him!" - Joseph Smith — History 1:17

Introduction

After asking of God, we need to be ready and willing to hear from Him. When we look to the few occurancies where the voice of the Father was heard, we will notice that his message is the same, he wants us to hear our Savior. The Father introduces the Savior, and bears witness of him. He repeatedly declared, "This is my beloved Son. Hear Him!"

When we come unto christ by learning, looking, and asking, we need to learn how to listen to what he has to say. His message for us will be personal, and we will not be able to hear him if we are not willing to obey. Christ will speak through the Holy Ghost in our hearts and our minds, but we will only be able to understand it if we have real intent.

We will be able to hear Christ when we seek him not to alter his will, but to receive. We will hear him when we are willing to act upon his personal intruction. And, once we hear him, we will be ready to walk beside him.

Prepare Spiritually

Ideas for Family Home Evening

Just as with any principle, spiritual knowledge is enlarged when we share it with others. We were instructed by living prophets to make our homes the main center of gospel learning.

  • Print the proclamation "The Restoration of the Fulness of the Gospel of Jesus Christ -- A Bicentennial Proclamation to the World", frame it (if possible), and put somewhere in your house where everyone can see it. Read it with your family, and poder about the consequences that followed the First Vision. Dicuss as a family what Joseph Smith did in order to be prepared to Hear the Savior. Ask each family member to write individually what each one of them can do to be more prepared to hear the Savior.
  • Create a simple obstacle course in your house. Take turns to go through the course blindefolded while other family members guide you through it without touching you. After each family member took a turn going through the course, liken this experience with Hearing the Savior. Ask them How does the Savior can guide us through this life's perils? How can we hear him better, and more often? Share a personal experience of when you heard a counsel from the Savior. Invite your family members to do the same.
  • Choose one of the accounts where God introduced Jesus as His Beloved Son. Read them together. Create a simple table with your family to describe these accounts. You can use the model below:
    Place Event Audience Message
    Acient Bountfill's temple After the land's destruction and darkness The Nephites He had glorified the Father in all things and fulfilled His will. Feel the tokens in his hands and feet, and thrust the hands in his side.
  • Ponder other ways that you can share what you learned in your study with your family. Remember, Family Home Evening is a great opportunity to teach the Gospel, but most importantly it is a time to disconnect and and spend time together as a family. More important than what you say, is what being felt and taught without words.
