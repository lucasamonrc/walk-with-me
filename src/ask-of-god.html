Week 4: Ask of God

"If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him."
- James 1:5

As we study the scriptures we need to be aware of thoughts that come to our mind. The Spirit will speak to our hearts and minds as we pay attention to our feelings and insights. We show appreciation to these promptings when we record them in a sacred place. Use the button below to create a note where you will record the impressions you have as you study this week's lesson.

As we learn from Christ and strive to follow his example, we will soon discover how difficult is the journey of the disicple. We will realize, however, how impossible life would be without it. In all of the hardships we will find along the way, the Lord promised to never forsake us.

Repeatedly he affirmed that he would leave the Comforter to help us. He taught that if we would ask anything to the Father in Christ's name, having faith, it would be given to us. One of the most fundamental truths of mortality is our right to receive guidance, and God's satisfaction in revealing His will to us.

As dark and dreary that life may seem sometimes, as terrible and confusing we may feel, there is one truth that we can hold on to. We can ask of God. We can ask guidance, we can ask help, we can ask questions. Our loving Father is desirous to grant us anything that is good if we should ask it of Him.

Prepare Spiritually

Throughout this week, study the following scripture passages to learn of him. Think of ways on how you can apply these scriptures to yourself and your family according to your needs. Pray for opportunities to share what you learned with others.

  • Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives (Pres. Russell M. Nelson)
  • Gospel Topics - Revelation
  • Matthew 7:7-11
  • 1 Corinthians 2:9-14
  • James 1:1-7
  • Moroni 10:3-5

Ideas for Family Home Evening

Just as with any principle, spiritual knowledge is enlarged when we share it with others. We were instructed by living prophets to make our homes the main center of gospel learning.
