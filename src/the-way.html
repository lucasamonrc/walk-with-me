Week 1: The Way

"(...), I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me."
- John 14:6

As we study the scriptures we need to be aware of thoughts that come to our mind. The Spirit will speak to our hearts and minds as we pay attention to our feelings and insights. We show appreciation to these promptings when we record them in a sacred place. Use the button below to create a note where you will record the impressions you have as you study this week's lesson.

We chose to come to this world with hope that here we would obtain a precious knowledge that would give our existance greater meaning and joy. We agreed in forget the happy memories we had about our pre-mortal life, and we subjected ourselves to live by faith. We knew that we would be tempted, hurt, deceived; but, we knew of the imense joy that awaited us as glorified being of flesh and bones. We also knew that not every one of us would come back home in the end.

Our Heavenly Father provided a Plan through His Son, Jesus Christ. This Plan would lead us directly back to Him if we would allow it to. Christ fulfilled the Father's plan, and he became the only way back to our Father. Only through him, we will be able to return home. Only through him, the trials of this life will have meaning. Only through him, we will overcome and be made whole to be welcomed back to our only home.

Jesus Christ, is The Way back to the Father. As we come to know him more perfectly, we will be inspired to do things that will direct us back home. No other doctrine, teaching, or individual will lead us safely back home, for he is "the way, the truth, and the life".

Prepare Spiritually

Throughout this week, study the following scripture passages to learn more about the way. Think of ways on how you can apply this scriptures to yourself and your family according to your needs. Pray for opportunities to share what you learned with others.

  • The Way of the Disciple (Elder Dieter F. Utchdorf; General Conference, April 2004)
  • Your Great Adventure (Elder Dieter F. Utchdorf; General Conference, October 2019)
  • John 14:1-5
  • 1 Nephi 8, 11
  • 2 Nephi 31
  • 3 Nephi 11, 27
