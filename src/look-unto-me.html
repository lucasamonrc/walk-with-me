Week 3: "Look Unto Me"

"Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not."
- D&C 6:36

As we study the scriptures we need to be aware of thoughts that come to our mind. The Spirit will speak to our hearts and minds as we pay attention to our feelings and insights. We show appreciation to these promptings when we record them in a sacred place. Use the button below to create a note where you will record the impressions you have as you study this week's lesson.

Jesus Christ is the perfect teacher. During his mortal ministry he taught spiritual principles that bring peace to this life and prepare us to eternal life. Christ wants us to live by the principles he taught so we may be transformed and become more like him. Christ did not live by a to-do list, nor did he constrain himself by specifically defining what good deeds he needed to do. Christ simply was what he was, and did good things because he is good.

Jesus did not provide us a comprehensive list of all things that we must do, neither did he prescribered a closed and restrict set of behaviors for us to follow. Instead, he gave us plain and simple principles to guide our lives. It is part of our mortal journey to learn how to live by these principles with the help of the Spirit and Christ's example.

Although Christ did not leave us a to-do list, he did leave his example. His life reflects all of the precious princples he taught, and we can learn to apply them in our lives if we simply look unto Him. Not only by knowing what he did, but sincerely studying his example, seeking to find him even in the small things. As we do, we will learn how to live the way he did.

Prepare Spiritually

Throughout this week, study the following scripture passages to learn of him. Think of ways on how you can apply these scriptures to yourself and your family according to your needs. Pray for opportunities to share what you learned with others.

  • We Look to Christ (Pres. Gordon B. Hinckley)
  • Becoming a Disciple of Our Lord Jesus Christ (Elder Robert D. Hales)
  • Mark 5:25-29
  • Alma 33:18-22
  • Doctrine and Covenants 6:36
